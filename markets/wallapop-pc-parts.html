Spanish marketplace guide

How to find PC parts on Wallapop

Wallapop is a major source of local second-hand technology listings in Spain. It can be useful for graphics cards, processors and complete PCs when buyers verify specifications and avoid vague “gaming” labels.

Search with Spanish component terms

Combine the exact model number with “tarjeta gráfica,” “procesador,” “placa base,” “memoria RAM,” “SSD,” “disco duro” or “componentes PC.” For complete computers, “PC gaming” and “ordenador gaming” may help, but insist on a full component list before comparing prices.

PCPartDeals surfaces supported external offers in hardware categories. Open the Wallapop listing to verify the current seller, price, location, delivery option and description.

Use local search intelligently

Wallapop's local focus can make collection practical in larger cities. A local meeting may allow a seller to demonstrate a complete PC or installed graphics card. Agree on the test before traveling. Check temperatures, stability, fans, ports and obvious physical damage.

If shipping is used, confirm protective packaging and current marketplace payment protection. A motherboard, hard drive or graphics card can be damaged by poor packing. Keep a record of the described condition and avoid off-platform arrangements that weaken dispute evidence.

Value complete PCs by their parts

Marketing labels are not specifications. Ask for the exact CPU generation, graphics card, motherboard, RAM configuration, storage and power supply. A system with an old high-tier processor may perform worse than a newer midrange build. Generic power supplies can also reduce the value and safety of an otherwise appealing PC.

Calculate the value of each reusable component and include any immediate upgrades. Missing Wi-Fi, small storage, single-channel RAM or a weak power supply can turn a cheap system into an expensive project.

Recognize weak listings

Be cautious when a seller uses only stock photos, cannot identify the product, refuses basic questions or pushes immediate payment. For GPUs, request a current benchmark. For storage, ask for health data. For CPUs and motherboards, confirm socket, BIOS and whether all memory channels were tested.
