Budget graphics card guide

Find a cheap GPU in Europe without buying a false bargain

The cheapest listing is not always the lowest-cost upgrade. A sensible budget GPU should provide enough performance, fit the system and include credible evidence that it works.

Browse GPU listingsGraphics card checklist

Set a performance target first

Define the games, resolution and frame-rate target before searching. A low-cost card for esports at 1080p has different requirements from a card for recent games or creative workloads. Check whether your monitor needs a particular output and whether your software benefits from NVIDIA or AMD-specific features.

Use several acceptable models rather than waiting for one exact card. Comparable alternatives make it easier to recognize real value and avoid overpaying because a popular model name attracts more buyers.

Calculate the complete upgrade cost

Add shipping, marketplace charges, power-supply upgrades and adapters. An older card may use substantially more electricity or require connectors your current PSU does not have. If the cooler needs new fans or the card is missing a bracket, include those costs.

Compare with a new entry-level GPU that includes a warranty. The used option should provide enough performance or savings to justify reduced protection. A small discount on an old card with uncertain history is not necessarily good value.

Use multiple European sources

Kleinanzeigen, Leboncoin and Wallapop can provide strong local inventory in Germany, France and Spain. Vinted, Yaga and Osta may expose additional component listings in supported regions. PCPartDeals brings supported offers into a PC-focused browse page so buyers can compare categories and prices.

Local collection may avoid shipping and allow a test. Cross-border offers can expand selection but may add delivery cost, language uncertainty and a more complicated return process.

Do not lower the safety standard

Cheap cards still need current photos and testing. Ask for temperatures, stable performance and any known defects. Avoid listings described as untested unless you intentionally want a repair project and the price reflects a high chance of failure.

Review seller history and keep payment on the original marketplace when protection depends on it. The GPU scam guide explains common warning signs, while the used GPU guide covers technical checks.

Frequently asked questions

How do I know whether a cheap GPU is good value?

Compare performance, total cost, condition evidence and warranty with several used and new alternatives.

Should I buy an untested graphics card?

Only as a deliberate repair-risk purchase. Untested hardware should not be priced like a confirmed working card.
