Graphics card buying guide

Find and compare used GPU deals in Europe

Used graphics cards can deliver more gaming performance per euro, but model naming, board variants, shipping and condition make direct comparison difficult. Use a consistent process before choosing an NVIDIA GeForce or AMD Radeon card.

Where used graphics cards appear

Europe does not have one universal second-hand hardware platform. Kleinanzeigen is a major source of local listings in Germany, Leboncoin serves France, and Wallapop is widely used in Spain. Vinted has expanded beyond fashion into many product categories, while Yaga and Osta can surface hardware in Estonia. Availability and buyer protection differ by country, so always read the original platform rules.

PCPartDeals aggregates supported listings to make discovery easier. It does not replace the source marketplace: the seller profile, current availability, final price and payment process should be verified on the external listing.

How to compare a used GPU correctly

Start with the exact GPU model and memory capacity. An RTX 3060 12GB is not directly equivalent to an RTX 3060 8GB, and laptop chips should not be compared with desktop cards. Board-partner coolers can affect noise, temperature and physical size, but condition matters more than cosmetic design.

  • Check the total cost including shipping and marketplace fees.
  • Confirm card length, slot thickness and required power connectors.
  • Ask whether the card was repaired, opened or modified.
  • Request a recent benchmark result that shows temperature and stability.
  • Compare performance with the games and resolution you actually use.

Mining, age and condition

A card previously used for mining is not automatically defective. Continuous operation at controlled temperatures can be less damaging than repeated overheating, but the seller should be transparent. Fan wear, dust, corrosion, damaged connectors and unstable memory are more useful warning signs than the mining label alone.

When buying remotely, request a photo showing the card and a handwritten date or marketplace username. If possible, ask for a short video of the card running under load. Do not move payment to an unfamiliar channel because a seller claims the marketplace fee is inconvenient.

Price versus warranty

Compare the used offer with a current new card, not only the original launch price. New entry-level cards may provide newer media engines, lower power use and a full warranty. A used card becomes attractive when its performance advantage and price difference are meaningful enough to justify the reduced warranty and uncertain history.

For more general component checks, read the used PC parts Europe guide. If an offer looks suspicious, review how to avoid GPU scams before contacting the seller.

Frequently asked questions

Is a used GPU worth buying?

It can be when the saving is meaningful, the model suits your system and the seller provides credible testing evidence.

What tests should a seller provide?

A recent benchmark or demanding game session should show stable performance, sensible temperatures and no artifacts or crashes.
